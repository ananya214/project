Laparoscopy is a minimally invasive surgical procedure used to examine and treat organs inside the abdomen or pelvis. It involves making small incisions through which a thin, lighted tube (laparoscope) and surgical instruments are inserted. This technique is commonly used for diagnosing and treating conditions related to the uterus, ovaries, fallopian tubes, appendix, gallbladder, and more. Laparoscopy offers faster recovery, less pain, and minimal scarring compared to traditional open surgery.
